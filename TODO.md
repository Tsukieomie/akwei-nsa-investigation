### 0a. Bone Conduction Audiometry as a Forensic Evidence Thread
**Tier:** 🟡 Laptop  
**Purpose:** Identify any documented case where a patient reporting transmitted speech / V2K symptoms requested bone-conduction audiometry and was denied, and any case where the test was performed.  
**Status:** Opened April 22, 2026 (Round 7)  
**Rationale:** The microwave auditory effect produces a real acoustic signal at the cochlea (distinguishable on bone-conduction audiometry); schizophrenic auditory hallucination does not. DIRD 26 documents that the paranoid-spectrum diagnosis is assigned *"ubiquitously"*, yet this cheap, non-invasive test that physically distinguishes the two hypotheses is not part of standard psychiatric workup.

**Sources to canvass:**
- Court records in civil commitment cases where the patient raised V2K claims
- State disability / SSA ALJ opinions (publicly indexed) mentioning auditory hallucinations and RF claims
- Patient advocacy networks for first-person accounts of requests for audiology referral
- Peer-reviewed literature: any paper that performed bone-conduction audiometry on a V2K claimant

**Success metric:** At least one documented denial and one documented test result.  
**Next step:** Legal-database search (Justia, CourtListener) for "bone conduction" + "hallucination" in mental-health proceedings.
