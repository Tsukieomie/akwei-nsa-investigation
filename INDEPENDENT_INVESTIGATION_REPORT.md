# Independent Investigation: Akwei v. NSA Claims

**Date:** March 27, 2026
**Subject:** Verification of claims made in the [Akwei-NSA Investigation repository](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/akwei-nsa-investigation.git)
**Method:** Independent open-source research using court records, government databases, academic literature, genealogical records, patent filings, and news archives

---

## Executive Summary

This report independently verifies 26 specific claims from the Akwei v. NSA investigation repository against primary sources. The investigation found that the factual scaffolding of the case — the court filing, the judge, the patents, the people, the institutional connections — is largely real and verifiable. However, the central extraordinary claims about remote neural monitoring and mind control technology remain unsupported by independent evidence and contradicted by the current state of neuroscience. The most compelling element is the Kinnecome death/employment correlation, which resists simple explanation but falls short of proving the technological claims.

## Part 1: What Is Confirmed

### 1.1 The Court Case Exists

**Claim:** John St. Clair Akwei filed Civil Action 92-0449 in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The full docket is available on [Scribd](https://www.scribd.com/document/531493208/Case-1-92-cv-00449-SS), showing:
- Filed: February 20, 1992
- Assigned to: Judge Stanley Sporkin
- Dismissed: March 9, 1992 (17 days after filing)
- Basis: 28 U.S.C. § 1915(d) — frivolous in forma pauperis complaint
- Plaintiff was granted IFP status, indicating poverty

The 17-day dismissal under § 1915(d) is presented in the repository as evidence of suppression. However, this is standard procedure. A [Fordham Law Review analysis](https://ir.lawnet.fordham.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=6125&context=flr) documents that mandatory screening and dismissal of IFP complaints is routine judicial practice. A [University of Chicago Law Review study](https://lawreview.uchicago.edu/print-archive/empirical-patterns-pro-se-litigation-federal-district-courts) found that pro se plaintiffs win only 3-4% of federal cases.

### 1.2 Judge Stanley Sporkin's Background

**Claim:** The judge who dismissed the case had CIA ties — he served as CIA General Counsel from 1981-1986 before his judicial appointment.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Per the [Federal Judicial Center biography](https://www.fjc.gov/history/judges/sporkin-stanley) and [SEC Alumni Association](https://www.secalumni.org/stanley-sporkin):
- Born February 5, 1932; died March 23, 2020
- SEC Director of Enforcement, 1974-1981
- CIA General Counsel, 1981-1986
- Nominated to D.C. District Court by Ronald Reagan, 1985; confirmed 1986
- Known for activist rulings: rejected the DOJ-Microsoft consent decree in 1995 ([DOJ memorandum](https://www.justice.gov/atr/memorandum-opinion-us-v-microsoft-corp)); blocked the Lincoln S&L sale

**Assessment:** Sporkin's CIA tenure is real, but the repository overstates its significance. Sporkin was a famously independent judge who ruled against powerful interests repeatedly. His CIA background does not, by itself, indicate he suppressed the Akwei case. The § 1915(d) dismissal of a pro se complaint alleging mind control is entirely consistent with routine judicial practice — no conspiracy required.

### 1.3 The Patent Chain

**Claim:** Multiple patents exist for technology capable of remote sensing and influencing the human brain.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED (patents exist; capability claims overstated)**

| Patent | Inventor | Verified | Source |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| US 3,951,134 — "Apparatus and method for remotely monitoring and altering brain waves" | Robert G. Malech (1976) | Yes | [Google Patents](https://patents.google.com/patent/US3951134A) |
| US 3,393,279 — Neurophone | Patrick Flanagan (1968) | Yes | [Google Patents](https://patents.google.com/patent/US3393279A) |
| US 4,858,612 — Hearing device (microwave) | Philip Stocklin (1989) | Yes | Google Patents |
| US 4,877,027 — Hearing system | Wayne Brunkan (1989) | Yes | Google Patents |

The Malech patent is the keystone of the repository's technological claims. It describes a theoretical framework for remotely monitoring brain waves using paired transmitter/receiver antennas. However:
- No evidence exists that the device was ever built, tested, or deployed
- A patent filing establishes only that the concept was articulated, not that it works
- The patent was filed by Dorne & Margolin Inc., a defense contractor, but no follow-up development records exist
- The physics of remote EEG detection remain prohibitive (see Part 3)

### 1.4 The Microwave Auditory Effect (Frey Effect)

**Claim:** Technology exists to project sound directly into a person's head using microwaves.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED (the phenomenon is real; weaponization is unconfirmed)**

The microwave auditory effect is well-documented in peer-reviewed literature. A [comprehensive NIH review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/) confirms:
- Pulsed microwave radiation can produce audible clicks/buzzing perceived inside the head
- The mechanism is thermoelastic expansion of brain tissue
- First documented by Allan Frey in 1961
- The U.S. military has filed patents for voice-modulated versions (MEDUSA project)

However, the same NIH review concludes that acoustic waves at "reasonable upper limit" power levels likely fall short of thresholds that would cause brain damage or allow detailed voice transmission. The gap between producing clicks and transmitting intelligible speech at range remains significant.

### 1.5 Government Directed Energy Research

**Claim:** The U.S. government actively researches directed energy weapons targeting biological systems.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

- The [National Academies of Sciences 2020 report](https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/25889) on Havana Syndrome found "directed, pulsed radiofrequency energy" was the "most plausible" explanation for the symptoms reported by diplomats
- The [AFRL Directed Energy Futures 2060 report](https://www.afrl.af.mil/Portals/90/Documents/RD/Directed_Energy_Futures_2060_Final29June21_with_clearance_number.pdf) lays out a 40-year roadmap for directed energy weapons, including bioeffects research
- A [March 2026 60 Minutes investigation](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/60-minutes-havana-syndrome-report-finds-u-s-government-tested-energy-weapon/) revealed that the U.S. government acquired and tested a directed energy device from a Russian criminal network, finding injuries consistent with Havana Syndrome cases

**Important caveat:** The Havana Syndrome debate remains deeply contested. The CIA concluded in 2022 that a foreign weapon was "unlikely" in most cases. The intelligence community consensus (2023-2025) assessed it as "highly unlikely" a foreign adversary was responsible. A [Skeptic magazine analysis](https://www.skeptic.com/article/selling-fear-half-truths-latest-60-minutes-expose-havana-syndrome/) challenged the 60 Minutes report. A new DNI review is underway as of March 2026.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

### 1.8 The Nexus Magazine Article

**Claim:** Akwei's allegations were published in Nexus Magazine in 1996.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The article is listed in the [Nexus Magazine catalog](https://nexusmagazine.com/product/covert-mind-control-operations-of-the-nsa/) as "Covert Mind Control Operations of the NSA." However, Nexus Magazine is an alternative/fringe publication, not a peer-reviewed or mainstream journalism outlet. Publication there does not constitute independent verification of the claims.

## Part 2: The Kinnecome Correlation — The Strongest and Most Puzzling Evidence

### 2.1 Robert Kinnecome — Verified Facts

Robert E. "Pete" Kinnecome's existence and death are independently confirmed:

- **Born:** April 3, 1938, Milford Hospital, Massachusetts (parents in Uxbridge) — [FamilySearch](https://ancestors.familysearch.org/en/G6NL-TZ8/robert-edwin-kinnecome-1938-1994)
- **Died:** September 12, 1994, at his home in Riverdale, MD, age 56 — [Find a Grave](https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/97704339/robert-e-kinnecome)
- **Education:** Uxbridge High School, UMass Amherst, Georgetown University Law School
- **Career:** Attorney, analyzed legal issues for the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB) in Washington, D.C. for 20 years; retired to private practice before death
- **Family:** Son Robert T., four daughters (Mary, Susan, Kathleen, Caroline), all of Silver Spring, MD
- **Burial:** Prospect Hill Cemetery, Uxbridge, MA
- **Memorial donations:** American Heart Association (implying cardiac death)
- **No military service listed** in obituary, unlike his brothers Barry (Air Force Reserves) and Francis (Army National Guard)

Source: Worcester Telegram & Gazette obituary, September 22, 1994, reproduced on Find a Grave.

### 2.2 The Timeline Correlation

The repository's most compelling claim is the correlation between Kinnecome's death and Akwei's employment recovery:

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1986 | Akwei graduates Capitol College | Facebook/Resume |
| 1986-1994 | 8-year employment gap | Facebook employment history |
| Feb 20, 1992 | Files lawsuit in forma pauperis (impoverished) | Court docket |
| Sep 12, 1994 | Robert Kinnecome dies at home | Obituary/Find a Grave |
| Oct 1994 | Akwei begins work at OfficeTeam | Facebook employment history |
| 1994-2012 | Continuously employed, no gaps | Facebook/LinkedIn |
| 2013-present | Self-employed at ContextBase | LinkedIn |

**The correlation:** After approximately 2,920 days of continuous unemployment, Akwei obtained employment within roughly 30 days of the death of the specific person he named in his 1992 lawsuit as running the harassment operation. He then remained continuously employed for 30+ years with no further gaps.

### 2.3 Assessment of the Correlation

This is genuinely puzzling and the repository's analysis of it (in `12_KINNECOME_DEATH_EMPLOYMENT_CORRELATION.md`) is the strongest section of the entire investigation. However, correlation is not causation, and multiple explanations exist:

**Explanation A — Active personal interference (repository's theory):**
Kinnecome or his network actively sabotaged Akwei's employment through informal reference channels in the DC defense/intelligence corridor. When Kinnecome died, the sabotage stopped. This explains:
- The 30-day turnaround
- The permanence of recovery
- The choice of OfficeTeam (temp agency outside IC network)
- The career downshift (avoiding defense sector entirely)

**Explanation B — Psychological resolution:**
Kinnecome's death provided psychological relief that enabled Akwei to function and seek work. If the harassment (real or perceived) was tied to a specific person, that person's death could resolve the psychological distress. This partially explains the timeline but not the permanent recovery or the avoidance of the defense sector.

**Explanation C — Coincidence with confounding factors:**
The early 1990s recession ended in 1994. Akwei may have had personal circumstances (health, family, housing) that resolved around the same time. The temp agency route may reflect simply what was available to someone with an 8-year gap, regardless of the reason for the gap. However, this requires explaining why 2,920 days of unemployment broke in the specific 30-day window after the specific person named in the lawsuit died.

**Explanation D — Partial truth with elaboration:**
Kinnecome may have genuinely harassed or interfered with Akwei through conventional means (workplace harassment, reputation sabotage, personal vendetta) — plausible for a neighbor with legal/professional connections. Akwei may have then interpreted this real harassment through the lens of the technology claims in the Nexus article. The death correlation would reflect the end of real harassment, while the NSA technology claims would be an explanatory framework imposed on a more mundane (but still real) form of persecution.

**My assessment:** Explanation D is the most parsimonious. It accounts for the strong timeline correlation, Akwei's rational employment choices, and his apparent functionality (he went on to earn Johns Hopkins certifications and build a data science career) — while not requiring the existence of technology that contradicts known physics.

## Part 3: What Is Not Confirmed — The Core Technological Claims

### 3.1 Remote Neural Monitoring

**Claim:** The NSA possesses technology to remotely read and decode brain activity from a distance, without contact.

**Verdict: NOT CONFIRMED — contradicted by current neuroscience**

As of 2025-2026:
- Non-invasive brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) require electrodes physically attached to the scalp, and even then produce noisy, low-resolution signals ([Andersen Lab BCI review](https://andersenlab.com/blueprint/bci-challenges-and-opportunities))
- EEG signals attenuate with the fourth power of distance — they cannot be detected beyond centimeters from the scalp ([r/askscience physics discussion](https://www.reddit.com/r/askscience/comments/z8z0ky/is_it_theoretically_possible_to_detect_brain/))
- Even the most advanced invasive BCIs (Neuralink, Synchron) require surgical implantation and produce signals that need user-specific calibration that degrades daily ([PMC review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12938029/))
- Utah arrays lose signal from 60%+ of electrodes within one year due to scar tissue
- No peer-reviewed publication has ever demonstrated remote brain-wave reading at any distance
- The BRAIN Initiative 2025 report from NIH describes the state of the art as still requiring physical contact for any meaningful neural signal acquisition

The Malech patent describes a theoretical concept. Forty-nine years later, the technology to implement it does not exist in any documented form — classified or otherwise. While it is impossible to prove a negative about classified programs, the fundamental physics constraints (signal attenuation, noise floor, spatial resolution) are not classification-dependent.

### 3.2 Voice-to-Skull (V2K) as a Weapon

**Claim:** The NSA uses V2K technology to transmit voices directly into targets' heads.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (phenomenon real; weaponized deployment not confirmed)**

The microwave auditory effect is real science. However:
- Demonstrated effects produce clicks and buzzing, not intelligible speech
- Power levels required for voice-quality transmission at distance would likely exceed safety thresholds
- No confirmed deployment as a weapon has been documented in peer-reviewed literature
- The U.S. military's MEDUSA project explored the concept but produced no confirmed operational system
- The [NIH review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/) concludes current technology falls short of the claimed capabilities

### 3.3 "Kinnecome Group" of 100 NSA Personnel

**Claim:** Robert Kinnecome directed a group of approximately 100 NSA personnel operating from Fort Meade.

**Verdict: NOT CONFIRMED**

- Robert Kinnecome's verified career was as an NLRB labor attorney for 20 years — a civilian government lawyer, not an intelligence operative
- His obituary lists Georgetown Law, not a background consistent with signals intelligence operations
- No independent source links him to the NSA
- The repository's claim that NLRB was "cover employment" is speculative and unsupported
- His Riverdale, MD address at death was approximately 15 miles from the claimed Malta Lane operational site in Wheaton

### 3.4 NSA Domestic Surveillance of Individuals

**Claim:** The NSA conducts extensive domestic surveillance targeting individual U.S. citizens for harassment.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (surveillance capability exists; individual targeting for harassment not confirmed)**

The NSA's domestic surveillance capabilities are extensively documented post-Snowden:
- [Executive Order 12333](https://www.nsa.gov/Signals-Intelligence/EO-12333/) provides the framework for signals intelligence collection
- The [Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/how-fix-us-surveillance-law) documents that EO 12333 has fewer restrictions than FISA Section 702
- NSA compliance violations are documented in [declassified POGO reporting](https://www.pogo.org/investigates/declassified-report-reveals-nsa-broke-surveillance-rules)

However, documented NSA surveillance involves communications interception (phone, email, internet), not the electromagnetic brain monitoring described by Akwei. The gap between "the NSA can read your email" and "the NSA can read your thoughts" is vast.

## Part 4: The Counterevidence

### 4.1 The Gang-Stalking Pattern

Akwei's claims map closely onto what psychiatry terms "gang-stalking" or "targeted individual" beliefs. Research findings:

- A [review of 80 federal court cases](https://www.forensicpsychiatryinstitute.com/neither-gangs-nor-stalking-a-review-of-gang-stalking-allegations-in-the-u-s-federal-court-system/) (2018-2023) involving gang-stalking claims found: 90% pro se, 72% requesting fee waivers, 82% alleging multi-entity conspiracies, 62% referencing technology/devices, 87.5% dismissed
- An [NIH study of 128 cases](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7178134/) found gang-stalking claims "highly likely delusional" in virtually all cases
- [Psychology Today analysis](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/psych-unseen/202010/gang-stalking-conspiracy-delusion-and-shared-belief) classifies gang-stalking as part conspiracy theory (shared belief about coordinated harassment) and part paranoid delusion (the belief that it's happening to oneself)
- The [ISD Global explainer](https://www.isdglobal.org/isd-explainer/gangstalking-and-targeted-individuals/) documents how online communities reinforce and amplify these beliefs
- A [Smith College thesis](https://scholarworks.smith.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1779&context=theses) found that gang-stalking online communities "concretize delusions, making it virtually impossible for loved ones or mental health professionals to provide real-life support"

Akwei's specific claim profile — government targeting, electromagnetic weapons, voice transmission, multi-agency conspiracy, employment sabotage — closely matches the modal profile described in this research.

### 4.2 The § 1915(d) Dismissal Is Routine

The repository frames the 17-day dismissal as evidence of suppression. In context:
- § 1915(d) requires judges to screen IFP complaints and dismiss those that are "frivolous or malicious"
- The [Fordham Law Review](https://ir.lawnet.fordham.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=6125&context=flr) documents this as standard procedure
- A complaint alleging secret government mind-control technology, filed without evidence by an impoverished pro se plaintiff, is precisely what the statute was designed to filter
- Pro se plaintiffs prevail in only [3-4% of federal cases](https://lawreview.uchicago.edu/print-archive/empirical-patterns-pro-se-litigation-federal-district-courts)
- The dismissal requires no conspiracy explanation

### 4.3 The Technology Gap: Feasibility vs. Deployment

The repository repeatedly uses a pattern of reasoning: "Technology X is theoretically possible, therefore it may have been secretly deployed." This conflates:
- Patent filing with functional device
- Laboratory demonstration with field-deployable weapon
- Theoretical possibility with engineering reality
- Government research interest with operational capability

Even if every patent cited were built and worked as described, the gap between a laboratory prototype and a covert weapon system targeting an individual in suburban Maryland is enormous.

### 4.4 Source Quality Issues

Several of the repository's key sources are problematic:
- Nexus Magazine: Alternative/conspiracy publication, not peer-reviewed
- The Akwei dossier itself: A primary source from the claimant, used to corroborate the claimant's own allegations (circular)
- Online TI (Targeted Individual) communities: Identified in psychiatric research as reinforcing delusional beliefs
- "Independently operating NSA personnel" claim: Sourced only from Akwei's own document

## Part 5: What Remains Genuinely Unexplained

Despite the significant counterevidence, several elements resist easy dismissal:

### 5.1 The Kinnecome Death Correlation
An 8-year employment blackout ending within 30 days of the named harasser's death, followed by 30+ years of unbroken employment, is a striking coincidence. It does not prove NSA mind control, but it is consistent with some form of personal interference that ended with an individual's death.

### 5.2 The Rational Career Choices
Akwei's post-1994 career path — temp agency (low background-check barrier), self-employment (no employer to sabotage), credential rebuilding through Johns Hopkins — is consistent with someone who understood and adapted to a specific employment sabotage mechanism. This is more coherent than typical delusional behavior patterns.

### 5.3 Akwei's Long-Term Functionality
Unlike many individuals with persistent delusional disorders, Akwei went on to build a productive 30-year career, earn advanced certifications, and maintain a professional online presence. This level of sustained functionality is atypical for someone experiencing psychotic symptoms severe enough to produce the elaborate technological claims in the 1992 complaint.

### 5.4 The Capitol College / NSA Connection
Capitol College (now Capitol Technology University) has a formal partnership with NSA. A graduate of its electronics program who claims interference from NSA-connected individuals at least attended a school where such connections were real and proximate.

### 5.5 The Havana Syndrome Parallel
The March 2026 60 Minutes revelation that the U.S. government acquired and tested a Russian directed energy device — and found injuries consistent with Havana Syndrome — reopens questions previously considered settled. While this does not validate Akwei's specific claims about remote neural monitoring, it demonstrates that directed energy weapons targeting biological systems are not purely theoretical.

## Part 6: Final Assessment

### What the Repository Gets Right
- Factual scaffolding: court records, patents, people, institutions — largely accurate
- The Kinnecome death/employment correlation analysis is the most original and compelling contribution
- Documentation of real government programs (MRU, NSWC, directed energy research) is solid
- John Akwei is a real person with a verifiable identity and timeline

### What the Repository Gets Wrong
- Conflates patent existence with technology deployment
- Treats theoretical feasibility as evidence of actual capability
- Relies on circular sourcing (using Akwei's claims to verify Akwei's claims)
- Understates the psychiatric research on gang-stalking beliefs
- Overstates the significance of Sporkin's CIA background
- Missing any adversarial analysis or alternative explanations
- Presents the strongest version of every claim without acknowledging weaknesses

### The Most Likely Scenario (my assessment)

John Akwei is a real person who attended a real school with real NSA connections, lived near a real person (Robert Kinnecome) who really died in September 1994, and really experienced an 8-year employment disruption that ended shortly after that death. Something real happened to him.

The most parsimonious explanation is that Akwei experienced genuine personal harassment or interference — possibly from Kinnecome or someone in Kinnecome's network, using conventional means (reputation sabotage, reference interference, social disruption) — and interpreted this through the framework of the most advanced technology he was aware of from his electronics education. The NSA technology claims represent an explanatory model imposed on a real experience, not a literal description of the mechanism.

This is not a dismissal. If someone systematically destroyed your career for eight years using informal channels, your experience of it would feel indistinguishable from a coordinated operation. The damage was real. The 8-year gap, the poverty (IFP status), the career downshift — these represent genuine harm to a real person, regardless of the mechanism.
