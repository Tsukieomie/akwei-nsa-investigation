**Search conducted:** 2025  
**Databases queried:** Google Patents, USPTO Patent Public Search (PPUBS), Espacenet, Lens.org  
**Date filter:** Filed or published 2010-01-01 or later (dual-cluster intersection); also forward citation searches on Coste 2010 and Flanagan US 3,393,279

## Executive Summary

A systematic patent search across four major patent databases, combining mechanosensitive ion channel terms (Piezo1, Piezo2, mechanosensitive channel, MSC, mechanotransduction, TRPV1, TRPV4, sonogenetics, Patapoutian) with directed-energy and weapons terms (directed energy, non-lethal, weapons, RF, microwave, pulsed RF, ultrasound, focused ultrasound, millimeter-wave, MEDUSA, active denial), yielded **10 substantive patent hits** combining both clusters.

**Category breakdown:**
- **Category A – Therapeutic / medical use:** 5 patents
- **Category B – Sonogenetics / focused-ultrasound neuromodulation:** 3 patents
- **Category C – Defense / non-lethal / weapons / surveillance:** 0 patents
- **Category D – Dual-use / ambiguous:** 1 patent
- **Category E – Pure mechanism / academic claims:** 1 patent

**Key negative finding:** No patent located — in any searched database — explicitly combines mechanosensitive ion channel biology (Piezo1, Piezo2, TRPV1/4, mechanotransduction) with weapons, directed-energy, non-lethal incapacitation, military, or surveillance language. The directed-energy weapon patents that exist in the pre-2010 corpus (MEDUSA/WaveBand, Raytheon Active Denial, Frey-effect devices) do not reference Piezo channels or mechanosensitive ion channel biology in any post-2010 citing patent found by this search.

**Cleanest signal:** The intersection of Piezo1/ultrasound neuromodulation and directed-energy concepts exists in the *scientific literature* (particularly the 2023 PNAS paper by Zhu et al.) but has not yet migrated into patent claims. The closest dual-use territory is [US20190308035A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20190308035A1/en) (abandoned) and [US20200384292A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20200384292A1/en) / US12186593B2 (granted), which claim focused-ultrasound activation of mechanosensitive channels for neuromodulation — capability with obvious dual-use potential, but no weapons language.

### Category B — Sonogenetics / Focused-Ultrasound Neuromodulation Patents

#### B1. US20190308035A1 — Non-Invasive Ultrasound Stimulation via Piezo1 (Abandoned)

**Patent Number:** US20190308035A1  
**Title:** A non-invasive method for selective neural stimulation by ultrasound  
**Assignee:** Not stated (academic origin; priority claim suggests UK institution)  
**Inventors:** Not listed on accessible page  
**Filing Date:** 2018-04-10  
**Publication Date:** 2019-10-10  
**Status:** **Abandoned**  
**URL:** [https://patents.google.com/patent/US20190308035A1/en](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20190308035A1/en)

**Summary:** A method for reversibly stimulating neurons by overexpressing mechanosensitive ion channels (specifically Piezo1, MscL-G22s, or CFTR) in target cells and then activating them with ultrasound pulses (0.1–0.6 MPa, 500 kHz), enabling non-invasive, spatially precise control of brain circuits.

**Key claims:**

> **Claim 1:** "A method for reversibly stimulating at least one or more neuronal cells, said method comprising activating mechanosensitive ion channels of at least one or more neuronal cells expressing said channels by exposing said cells to an ultrasound stimulus."
>
> **Claim 2:** "The method…wherein the mechanosensitive ion channels are selected from the group comprising Piezo1, MscL-G22s, CFTR."
>
> **Claim 13:** "A method of increasing neuron sensitivity to ultrasound by overexpression therein of at least one or more proteins selected from the group comprising piezol, MscL-G22s, CFTR, wherein the overexpression is by introducing genetic material encoding the at least one or more proteins in the neuronal cells by transfection with a plasmid or a virus..."

**Passage linking mechanosensitive channels to directed ultrasound energy:**
> "Ultrasound triggered mechanosensitive ion channels as described herein addresses the need in the art for non-invasive, precisely controllable, wide field of view stimulation of neurons and neural circuits for understanding advanced brain function and developmental problems."
>
> "the present disclosure identified ultrasound-sensitive cellular machinery that can respond to stimulation and consequently initiate neural activity and signalling, granting precise control of mammalian neurons and animal behaviour."

**Experimental data from description:** Piezo1 overexpression in M1 motor cortex of mice; ultrasound at 0.3 MPa induced tail flick in 39% of Piezo1-transfected mice vs. 11% controls; head movement rate 51% vs. 31%. Demonstrates remote, non-contact behavioral control of animals via ultrasound acting through Piezo1.

**Defense/weapons language:** None. However, the capability described — non-invasive remote control of neuronal circuits and animal behavior via ultrasound acting through Piezo1 — is the technical basis from which a non-lethal behavioral control application could theoretically be built. This is dual-use-capable science in a therapeutic patent frame, which is why it is also noted in the Notable Absences section.

However, no such language appears in this patent.

**Patent Number:** US8049173B1  
**Title:** Dual use RF directed energy weapon and imager  
**Assignee:** Raytheon Company  
**Inventor:** Kenneth W. Brown  
**Filing Date:** 2007-05-17  
**Publication Date:** 2011-11-01 (published within search window)  
**Status:** Active, expires 2030-08-31  
**URL:** [https://patents.google.com/patent/US8049173B1/en](https://patents.google.com/patent/US8049173B1/en)

**Summary:** A man-portable directed-energy weapon/imager combination using W-band millimeter-wave (75–110 GHz) electromagnetic energy as the directed-energy beam, plus an imaging subsystem; uses active denial (heating) principle for target incapacitation. The weapon is designed to "deter or discourage" a target via directed millimeter-wave energy, which causes a burning sensation.

**Key claim language:**
> "a generator may provide a first beam of RF electromagnetic energy which may be directed at an object to incapacitate, deter or impede the object or to otherwise prevent the object from performing some desired action"

**Classification:**
> F41H13/0068 — "Directed energy weapons, i.e. devices that direct a beam of high energy content toward a target for incapacitating or destroying the target — the high-energy beam being of microwave type, e.g. for causing a heating effect in the target"

**Mechanosensitive channel language:** **None.** The active denial heating effect is mediated by TRPV1 (the capsaicin/heat receptor, which responds to temperatures above 43°C) and has been proposed to involve TRP channels in the biological literature, but the patent text does not mention mechanosensitive channels, Piezo, mechanotransduction, TRPV, or any ion channel biology.

**Reason for Category D inclusion:** This is the most prominent post-2010 directed-energy weapon patent in the database search. It is classified D (dual-use/ambiguous) solely because the millimeter-wave active denial mechanism is known from the scientific literature to activate heat-sensitive TRP channels — a mechanosensitivity adjacent mechanism — yet this biology is absent from the claims. The patent is not a Piezo/mechanosensitive channel patent. It is listed here in full transparency as the most significant negative comparison: a weapons patent that uses directed electromagnetic energy for human behavioral effect, but which does not invoke mechanosensitive channel biology in any claim or description element.

## Notable Absences

The following combinations were explicitly searched and **not found:**

1. **Piezo1 or Piezo2 + weapon, non-lethal, incapacitation, active denial, crowd control, MEDUSA, military, DARPA, defense:** Zero hits across all databases. The Piezo channel literature (2010–2025) has generated thousands of citations and dozens of patents, none of which use weapons or defense language in claims or descriptions.

2. **Sonogenetics + non-lethal / weapon / behavioral control / surveillance:** Zero hits. The sonogenetics field (Ibsen 2015, Qiu 2019, Shen 2021, Zhu 2023, Chen group TRPV1 work) is entirely couched in therapeutic neuromodulation. No patent in this field claims any defense application.

3. **TRPV1 / TRPV4 + directed energy + weapon:** Zero hits. TRPV1 is activated by the Raytheon Active Denial System (95 GHz, 3.2mm wavelength) per the scientific/military literature, but this is not stated in any patent text found.

4. **Millimeter-wave + mechanosensitive ion channel + neural stimulation (any use):** Zero hits. A 2011 NASA Tech Brief describes a millimeter-wave experiment that may depolarize neuronal membranes via a membrane mechanism (FRET signal at 50 GHz), but no patent was filed on this finding.

5. **Pulsed RF + Piezo / mechanosensitive + behavioral modification:** Zero hits.

6. **Chinese military/PLA patents combining Piezo1/Piezo2 + directed energy:** None located via English-language patent database searches. The Guangdong Institute of Intelligence Science and Technology (which produced the Zhu et al. 2023 PNAS paper) has not filed discoverable patents combining Piezo neuromodulation with directed-energy applications.

7. **Any patent citing Coste 2010 (Piezo1/Piezo2 discovery) that uses weapons, military, or directed-energy language:** Zero. The Coste 2010 paper has been cited thousands of times in the scientific literature. Forward-citation via Google Patents and patent database searches found only therapeutic, drug delivery, and basic science patents citing this foundational work. None of the citing patents involve defense applications.

## Analyst Notes

### The Gap Between Science and Patent Claims

The science strongly supports that Piezo1 (and to a lesser degree Piezo2, TRPV1, TREK-1, TRAAK, MscL) are activated by focused ultrasound. The 2023 PNAS paper by Zhu et al. demonstrated that knocking out Piezo1 in the mouse motor cortex abolished ultrasound-induced limb movement. The 2022 PNAS paper by Hoffman et al. demonstrated that PIEZO2 sets the threshold for FUS activation of peripheral sensory neurons. These findings have direct implications for any non-invasive behavioral control technology using ultrasound.

Yet the patent literature shows a striking gap: no inventor — academic, commercial, or defense — has filed a patent that explicitly connects Piezo channel biology to directed acoustic energy in a non-therapeutic or defense context. This gap could reflect:

1. **Classification strategy:** Defense researchers may be filing in classified patent systems (e.g., DTIC, classified USPTO channels) not searchable by public databases.
2. **Technology immaturity:** The Piezo1/ultrasound neuromodulation mechanism was confirmed at behaviorally relevant levels only in 2019–2023; the patent lag is expected to be 2–5 years.
3. **Genuine absence:** No defense program has yet pursued this specific pathway.

The PIRF patent (US12186593B2) is the most technically advanced non-therapeutic neuromodulation patent found, and while it names Piezo channels in the description, it is framed entirely as a medical device. The Apex Neuro patents name Piezo2 as the target mechanoreceptor for a wearable anxiety treatment device. None of these crosses into defense territory.

### Piezoelectric in Weapons ≠ Piezo Channels in Weapons

A search artifact worth noting: US10408579 (Army, 2019) and US20080289531A1 use "piezo" or "piezoelectric" in a weapons context — specifically, a piezoelectric generator integrated into a blank firing adapter for an M4 carbine to generate pulse power, and a piezoelectric stun projectile respectively. These have no relationship to Piezo1/Piezo2 ion channels. The term "piezo" in weapons patents almost always refers to piezoelectric transducers (energy generators), not mechanosensitive ion channels. This distinction is critical.
