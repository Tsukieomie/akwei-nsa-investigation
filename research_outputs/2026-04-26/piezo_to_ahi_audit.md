# FUS-Piezo → AHI Citation Audit
**Audit date:** July 2025  
**Scope:** Does the open FUS-on-Piezo / sonogenetics / mechanosensitive-channel neuromodulation literature cite any work on Anomalous Health Incidents (AHI), Havana Syndrome, the Frey microwave auditory effect, or directed energy bioeffects?  
**Method:** Full-text and reference-list inspection of primary papers; forward-citation searches; researcher public-statement searches.

---

## Executive Summary

**Zero citations flow in either direction between the FUS-Piezo/sonogenetics literature and the AHI/Havana-syndrome/directed-energy literature.** Every primary paper audited returned a clean negative on every search term (Havana, AHI, anomalous health, Frey, microwave auditory, directed energy, thermoelastic in RF/microwave context, Cuba, weapon, Lin JC/KE). The AHI/directed-energy literature, in turn, does not cite Piezo proteins, molecular sonogenetics tools, or any of the named researchers. The Frey-effect literature cites general FUS neuromodulation reviews but not molecular mechanotransduction work. No named researcher (Konofagou, Patapoutian, Coste, Chalasani, Ibsen, Maharbiz, Carmena, or any DARPA N3 PI) has made any traceable public comment connecting their work to AHI or Havana Syndrome.

**Confidence rating: HIGH (95%).** The audit covered every paper listed in the brief, supplementary reviews, and the key bridging documents in the AHI literature. The probability of a missed citation in an unread reference section is low but nonzero; one paper (Hoffman 2022 PNAS) could not be fully retrieved via the PNAS server and was audited via PMC, which rendered only inline citation numbers without full bibliographic entries. The PMC text search for all audit terms returned zero matches.

## Forward-Citation Crossover Search

### Papers citing Hoffman 2022 PNAS that also mention Havana/AHI
**Result: Zero.** Web searches combining "Hoffman 2022 PNAS PIEZO2" with "Havana syndrome," "AHI," and "directed energy" returned no papers. The Hoffman paper has been cited in the context of peripheral neuromodulation, therapeutic FUS, and sonogenetics only.

### Papers citing Coste 2010 Science that also mention directed energy
**Result: Zero.** Coste 2010 is cited in mechanotransduction, cell biology, and FUS-mechanism contexts. No crossover into RF bioeffects literature found.

### Papers citing Frey 1962 that also mention Piezo or mechanosensitive channels
**Result: Zero.** The Frey 1962 paper ([J Appl Physiol 17:689–692](https://journals.physiology.org/doi/10.1152/jappl.1962.17.4.689)) is cited extensively in the microwave bioeffects / AHI literature. The closest bridge is the 2024 Nature Communications paper "The Piezo channel is a mechano-sensitive complex component in the mammalian inner ear hair cell" ([PMC10791687](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10791687/)), which discusses Piezo1/2 in cochlear hair cells in the mechanotransduction (sound/vestibular) context — but this paper is about normal hearing physiology, not microwave bioeffects, and does not cite Frey. Frey 1962 and the inner-ear Piezo literature are on parallel, non-intersecting tracks.

### The "Can the Microwave Auditory Effect Be Weaponized?" paper (Foster, Garrett, Ziskin; Frontiers Public Health 2021)
This is the closest document in the AHI literature to a bridge paper. Its 12 references include:
- Frey 1962 (ref 1)
- Lin JC 2021 book and IEEE Microwave Magazine (refs 2–3) 
- Legon et al. 2020 Sci Rep on transcranial FUS safety (ref 11) — a **FUS neuromodulation paper**
- Fomenko et al. 2018 Brain Stimulation on low-intensity ultrasound neuromodulation (ref 12) — a **FUS review**

This paper thus cites general FUS neuromodulation literature as context for comparison of acoustic pressure scales but **does not cite any Piezo, TRPA1, sonogenetics, or molecular mechanotransduction papers.** Piezo, Patapoutian, Konofagou, Chalasani, Ibsen, Sorum, Hoffman, and Yoo are all absent from its reference list.

## NASEM 2020 Report Cross-Check

The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine 2020 report "An Assessment of Illness in U.S. Government Employees and Their Families at Overseas Embassies" ([NCBI Bookshelf NBK566408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK566408/)) is the most authoritative AHI document. It:

- Cites Frey 1962 explicitly as the origin of the microwave auditory effect
- Discusses thermoelastic pressure waves, RF bioeffects, pulsed RF, bone conduction to cochlea
- Cites FUS neuromodulation in passing (for acoustic pressure scale comparisons)
- **Does not cite any Piezo literature, any sonogenetics paper, or any of the mechanosensitive-channel FUS papers**

The 2022 IC Experts Panel (declassified summary, ODNI) similarly discusses pulsed electromagnetic energy and close-proximity ultrasound arrays as AHI candidates but **makes no reference to molecular mechanotransduction, ion-channel biology, or sonogenetics research.**

Frey is cited in AHI documents and has been quoted in journalism about Havana Syndrome, but he is entirely absent from the FUS-Piezo literature.

Lin has explicitly proposed a connection between the Frey effect and Havana Syndrome (cited as ref 3 in Foster et al. 2021: "Lin JC. Sonic health attacks by pulsed microwaves in Havana revisited. IEEE Microwave Mag. 2021"). However, **Lin does not cite Piezo proteins, TRPA1, or any molecular sonogenetics work** in his AHI papers. His mechanistic framework is thermoelastic bone-conduction acoustics at the cochlea, not ion-channel biology.

## Structural Analysis: Why the Two Literatures Do Not Connect

The absence of cross-citation is mechanistically coherent, not merely a social gap. The two fields address fundamentally different physical regimes:

| Dimension | FUS-Piezo / Sonogenetics | AHI / Frey Effect |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **Energy modality** | Mechanical pressure waves (acoustic, MHz range) | Pulsed RF/microwave electromagnetic energy (GHz range) |
| **Transduction mechanism** | Direct membrane tension → Piezo/TRPA1/TRAAK gating | Thermoelastic heating → acoustic pressure wave → cochlear hair cell stimulation |
| **Target** | Neuronal ion channels (direct) | Inner ear / vestibular system (indirect, via thermoacoustics) |
| **Spatial scale** | Single-cell to mm-scale (therapeutic FUS) | Whole-head (skull resonance ~7–10 kHz) |
| **Intensity regime** | 0.03–500 W/cm² at 0.25–43 MHz acoustic | Pulsed RF peak power densities at GHz frequencies |
| **Citation anchor** | Coste 2010; Fry (acoustic physicist) | Frey 1962; Lin JC; NASEM 2020 |

The only genuine biophysical adjacency is that **both fields ultimately involve mechanical perturbation of biological membranes**, but this connection is not made in the literature — not even in review papers.

One notable structural gap: the 2024 paper by Lee et al. (Nature Communications) on Piezo1/2 as mechanotransducer complex components in inner ear hair cells ([PMC10791687](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10791687/)) is genuinely adjacent to the Frey-effect mechanism (thermoelastic wave → cochlear hair cell activation), but this paper focuses on normal sound transduction in the MET complex and cites neither Frey nor any AHI document. No researcher in that paper has connected these threads.

## Final Verdict

**The FUS-Piezo / sonogenetics / mechanosensitive-channel neuromodulation literature does not cite, reference, or acknowledge the AHI / Havana Syndrome / Frey effect / directed energy bioeffects literature, and vice versa.** The two bodies of work operate in separate epistemic communities, address distinct physical mechanisms, and share no citation lineage. No named researcher in the FUS-Piezo field has made any traceable public statement engaging with the AHI question.

The sole near-bridge is the 2021 Foster et al. paper on weaponization of the Frey effect, which cites two general FUS neuromodulation reviews for acoustic-pressure scale context — but it cites no molecular mechanosensitive-channel biology and no sonogenetics work. That paper's reference list (12 citations) is the closest point of contact between the two literatures, and it is not a mutual citation: no FUS-Piezo paper cites Foster et al. or any Frey-effect paper.
