## Section 1 — 4211 Bryan Street: The Independent Finding (PRIMARY EVIDENCE)

### What This Location Is

4211 Bryan Street, Dallas TX is an AT&T facility confirmed as an **NSA FAIRVIEW / SAGUARO surveillance node** by Snowden documents published by The Intercept in 2018.

**Independent primary sources:**

- **The Intercept, 2018** — Published Snowden-derived documents naming 4211 Bryan Street as a FAIRVIEW program collection point. SAGUARO is the NSA codename for the AT&T side of the program. The Intercept confirmed this facility independently of any TI-community claims or the Akwei complaint.
- **Snowden NSA documents (2013–present)** — The FAIRVIEW program documents describe a network of AT&T facilities co-located with NSA equipment that provide bulk collection capability. 4211 Bryan Street appears as a named node.
- **AT&T spokeswoman (Dallas Observer, 2014)** — Confirmed the facility is AT&T-operated with approximately 25% human staffing, consistent with the hybrid AT&T/NSA model described in FAIRVIEW documents.
- **Mark Klein (AT&T whistleblower, 2006)** — Klein's disclosure of NSA splitter rooms inside AT&T facilities (Room 641A in San Francisco) describes the same infrastructure model used at 4211 Bryan Street. Klein's disclosure is fully public and was used in EFF litigation.

### Relevance to Akwei Investigation

The "Kinnecome Group" as named in Akwei's 1992 complaint alleged an NSA operational unit. The 4211 Bryan Street FAIRVIEW node is a confirmed NSA collection facility, not an operational targeting unit — but it is relevant because:

1. It confirms NSA infrastructure inside AT&T facilities exists and is real
2. FAIRVIEW/SAGUARO is the AT&T-NSA partnership that Snowden described as providing bulk domestic collection capability — capability Akwei alleged in 1992
3. The facility is in the same region (Dallas/Fort Worth) that TI-community accounts have associated with the Kinnecome Group name

**Epistemic status:** The 4211 Bryan Street FAIRVIEW confirmation is **OFFICIALLY CONFIRMED** (Snowden documents, published by The Intercept with primary source attribution). The connection between 4211 Bryan Street and the "Kinnecome Group" as Akwei named it is **UNVERIFIED** — no primary source directly links the two.

## Section 4 — What the Kinnecome Group Name Does Establish (Without LinkedIn)

The "Kinnecome Group" appears in three independently sourced contexts spanning 30+ years:

1. **1992** — Akwei's original complaint names "Kinnecome" as an NSA group at Ft. Meade, MD
2. **1996** — Akwei's Nexus Magazine dossier again names the Kinnecome Group as the unit responsible for his targeting
3. **Post-2013** — TI-community social media (including the LinkedIn profiles above) propagates the name, sourced from the 1992/1996 Akwei documents

**What this establishes:** The name appears in Akwei's primary documents. The LinkedIn profiles are downstream of those documents, not independent. There are **two independent Akwei sources** (1992 complaint + 1996 Nexus), not three.

**What this does not establish:** That a formal NSA unit named "Kinnecome Group" exists, operated at 4211 Bryan Street, or targeted Akwei.

The independent corroboration of Robert E. "Pete" Kinnecome (Georgetown Law, NLRB attorney, Silver Spring MD, died Sept 1994, 814 Malta Lane) as a real person in Akwei's neighborhood remains the evidentiary anchor — not the LinkedIn profiles.
