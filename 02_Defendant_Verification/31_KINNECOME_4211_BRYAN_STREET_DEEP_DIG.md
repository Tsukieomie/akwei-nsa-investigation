## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This round examined the specific claim in Phillip Young's LinkedIn profile that the NSA Kinnecome Group "headquarters in Texas" operates from 4211 Bryan Street, Dallas/Fort Worth. The investigation reached a methodologically important fork: **4211 Bryan Street is independently confirmed as a real NSA signals collection node** (The Intercept, 2018); however, the Phillip Young LinkedIn profiles appear to be **TI-community harassment/doxxing profiles** that sourced their language from the 1992 Akwei complaint rather than constituting independent corroboration of Kinnecome Group operations at that address.

This distinction matters enormously for the investigation's evidentiary integrity.

## SECTION 1: 4211 Bryan Street — What Is Independently Confirmed

### Source: The Intercept (June 25, 2018)
**URL**: https://theintercept.com/2018/06/25/att-internet-nsa-spy-hubs/  
**Authors**: Ryan Gallagher and Henrik Moltke  
**Basis**: Classified AT&T documents provided by NSA whistleblower

### Confirmed Facts About 4211 Bryan Street:
1. **Physical address**: 4211 Bryan Street, Dallas, Texas — confirmed as an AT&T-owned building
2. **Program designation**: Part of NSA's **FAIRVIEW** program — a bulk surveillance partnership with AT&T running since 1985
3. **Facility type**: One of eight **"Service Node Routing Complexes" (SNRCs)** — peering points where domestic internet traffic is intercepted
4. **Codename**: **SAGUARO** (AT&T-specific component of FAIRVIEW)
5. **Operational division**: Managed by NSA's **Special Source Operations (SSO)** division
6. **Data collected**: Emails, web browsing, voice calls, VOIP — both domestic and international traffic flowing through AT&T peering infrastructure
7. **Classification**: The building's role was classified; AT&T employees working there had NSA-issued security clearances
8. **NOT an NSA campus**: NSA's official Texas facility is the **Texas Cryptologic Center (TCC)** at Lackland Air Force Base, San Antonio — approximately 280 miles from Dallas

### Geographic Note:
The address is in Dallas proper, but the surrounding metro area includes Fort Worth (~30 miles west). References to "Fort Worth, Dallas Texas" on the Young profiles appear to use "Fort Worth" as a regional descriptor for the DFW metroplex, not a separate Fort Worth facility.

### IRP/FAS Historical Note:
A Federation of American Scientists / Intelligence Resource Program document notes that in **summer 1994**, NSA advertised employment in a **Dallas-area newspaper** with references to **Cray C916 supercomputers** — suggesting operational infrastructure in the Dallas area predating or concurrent with the SAGUARO buildout. This ad is consistent with staffing a signals processing facility such as 4211 Bryan Street.

### Critical Assessment

**Who created this profile?**

The profiles were NOT created by Phillip Young. The language pattern, content structure, and PII publication are characteristic of **"targeted individual" (TI) community harassment** — a documented online phenomenon where individuals who believe they are victims of government surveillance programs publish profiles, websites, and posts identifying people they believe are involved in targeting them.

Key indicators:
- Publishing a target's home address, mother's name, mother's city, and age — this is doxxing, not whistleblowing
- The "Kinnecome Group," "remote neural monitoring," and "mind control" terminology is drawn **verbatim** from the 1992 Akwei v. NSA complaint, which circulates heavily in TI communities
- Profile 2's title "TS NSA Mind Control" directly mirrors Akwei's complaint language
- The profiles have 1 follower and minimal activity — consistent with harassment profiles, not genuine professional accounts
- No skills, recommendations, or professional network — not a functioning LinkedIn profile

**What this means for the investigation:**

| Claim | Evidentiary Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 4211 Bryan Street is an NSA surveillance node | **CONFIRMED** (independent) | The Intercept, 2018, Snowden documents |
| Kinnecome Group operated at 4211 Bryan Street | **UNCONFIRMED** | LinkedIn profiles sourced from Akwei complaint; circular |
| Phillip Young is an NSA employee | **UNCONFIRMED** | Profiles appear to target him, not document him |
| "Fort Worth Texas NSA spy Hub" language | **PARTIALLY CONSISTENT** | Geographically coherent; terminology from Akwei complaint |

**The circular evidence problem:**

The LinkedIn profiles cite "Kinnecome Group at 4211 Bryan Street" — but the source of this claim is the Akwei complaint itself. The profiles do NOT constitute independent corroboration. They are Akwei-derived content applied to a real person, likely by someone who:
1. Read the Akwei complaint (widely available online)
2. Believes the Kinnecome Group is real and operational
3. Identified Philip Young as someone connected to NSA surveillance operations
4. Created harassment profiles targeting Young

This means the Phillip Young LinkedIn should be treated as **evidence of the Akwei complaint's reach within the TI community**, not as independent verification of Kinnecome Group operations.

## SECTION 3: What Remains Independently Valid

Despite the LinkedIn profiles being TI-community harassment content, the following facts from this investigation remain independently grounded:

### A. The Three-Source Kinnecome Group Timeline
The name "Kinnecome Group" appears in three independent contexts across 30 years:

| Year | Source | Content | Independence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1992 | Akwei v. NSA complaint | "MR. KINNECOME (Retired N.S.A. agent)" + named unit conducting RNM | Primary source; pre-internet |
| 1996 | Nexus article quoting Akwei | "The Kinnecome group has about 100 persons working 24-hours-a-day at Ft Meade" | Secondary but quotes Akwei directly |
| ~2018–2019 | Phillip Young LinkedIn | "NSA Kinnecome Group located at Fort Worth, Dallas Texas" | Downstream of Akwei complaint; not independent |

**Revised assessment**: Two independent sources (1992 + 1996), both tracing to Akwei. The LinkedIn is not a third independent source.

### B. Robert "Pete" Kinnecome — The Person Behind the Name
- Named in Akwei's 1992 complaint as "Retired N.S.A. agent"
- Obituary research: Georgetown Law graduate; NLRB attorney ~20 years
- FamilySearch: Died September 12, 1994, **Severna Park, Anne Arundel County, Maryland** — 8 miles from NSA Fort Meade
- Family address: 814 Malta Lane, Silver Spring MD — one block from Akwei's own address (819 Malta Lane)
- This geographic proximity is **independently verified** and does not depend on the LinkedIn profiles

### C. 4211 Bryan Street — Confirmed Independently
- The Intercept's reporting is based on classified AT&T documents from Snowden archive
- This confirmation stands entirely independent of the Akwei complaint or TI-community content
- The NSA SSO/FAIRVIEW/SAGUARO framework is documented in primary source materials

### D. The NSA Texas Geography Problem
- NSA's official Texas presence: Texas Cryptologic Center (TCC), Lackland AFB, **San Antonio** — 280+ miles from Dallas
- 4211 Bryan Street is an **AT&T-owned, NSA-operated SNRC** — not an official NSA campus
- This means any NSA personnel operating there would appear on organizational charts as AT&T or as NSA SSO contractors, not as "NSA Dallas employees"
- The lack of visible NSA presence in Dallas is **consistent with a classified covert node**, not evidence against one

## SECTION 5: Methodological Note for Investigation Integrity

This investigation must maintain a strict evidentiary standard. The Phillip Young LinkedIn profiles:
- Contain real geographic facts (4211 Bryan Street is real) mixed with Akwei-derived claims
- Appear to target a real private individual through doxxing
- Should NOT be cited as independent corroboration of Kinnecome Group operations

**The distinction**: Akwei's original complaint is the primary source for "Kinnecome Group." Everything downstream of it — including the LinkedIn profiles — reflects the complaint's circulation, not independent verification.

This does not undermine the investigation. The original complaint's specificity (naming a neighbor, naming a unit, naming an address) remains the core evidentiary foundation. The goal is to find primary sources that either corroborate or contradict Akwei's account — not to cite circular references back to his own complaint.
