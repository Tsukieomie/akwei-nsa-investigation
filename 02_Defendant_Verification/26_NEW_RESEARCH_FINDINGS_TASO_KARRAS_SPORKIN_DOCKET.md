**Service Period:** December 17, 1985 - January 15, 2000 (14 years on D.C. District bench)
- Appointed by Reagan (April 5, 1985), Confirmed (December 16, 1985)
- Entered senior status (February 12, 1999)

**Intelligence/National Security Cases Documented (1992-2000):**
1. **Akwei v. NSA** (1992) - ONLY documented NSA case during this period
2. **FOIA/Administrative Cases** - Late 1997 FOIA order criticizing FBI's "muscle-bound" structure; ordered expedited processing
3. **Fourth Amendment Cases** - General criminal motions (not intelligence-specific)

**Other Notable Cases (Same Period):**
1. **Turner Broadcasting v. FCC** (1993) - First Amendment/cable regulation

- **In Akwei (1992):** Sporkin was perfunctory - 17-day dismissal, no analysis, no NSA response required

**Implication:**
Judge Sporkin *demonstrated capability* for aggressive, probing judicial review in other contexts, making the rapid Akwei dismissal more conspicuous. He chose not to engage with the merits, despite his established pattern of deep involvement in complex cases.

### Absence of Documented Intelligence Cases

**Notable Finding:** No documented cases found involving Sporkin ruling on:
- State secrets privilege assertions

- Classified information disclosure litigation
- FOIA requests for classified NSA documents

This absence is significant because the D.C. District Court is *precisely* where such cases are filed, yet Akwei appears to be the *only* NSA intelligence case Sporkin encountered during his 14-year tenure (1985-2000).

### Conflict of Interest Status
