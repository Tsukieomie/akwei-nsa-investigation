## Section 1: Directed Energy Futures 2060 -- Official DoD Validation

**Document:** "Directed Energy Futures 2060: Visions for the next 40 years of U.S. Department of Defense Directed Energy technologies"
**Author:** Office of the U.S. Air Force's Chief Scientist for Directed Energy, Air Force Research Laboratory

**Classification:** Approved for public release; distribution unlimited. Public Affairs release approval # AFRL-2021-1152

**Participants:** DoD, DoE, NASA, NATO, industry, academia

### Why This Is the Single Most Important Document in the Investigation

This is not a leaked document, a conspiracy theory website, or a whistleblower account. This is an **official, publicly released U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory report** written for the Secretary of Defense, describing Directed Energy Weapons as real, deployed, proliferating, and militarily critical. It validates virtually every technological claim Akwei made in 1992.

### Key Findings from the Report

**1. Directed Energy Weapons Are Real and Deployed**
The report states the DoD has reached or passed a "tipping point" for the criticality of DE capabilities. As of 2020:
- 31 independent nations have built 321 EW or HPM DEWs for counter-UAS missions alone
- Ground-based, maritime, airborne, and space-based DE platforms are all operational
- The U.S., Russia, and China all have non-lethal ADS-like counter-personnel systems

**2. High Power Microwave (HPM) Weapons -- The Core Technology**
- HPM weapons generate electromagnetic radiation from 300 MHz to 300 GHz
- Peak power exceeds 100 Megawatts
- R&D began in the 1970s -- the EXACT decade preceding Akwei's claims
- Gigawatt vacuum-tube HPM systems have been demonstrated
- Semiconductor HPM systems are compact (meters in size, hundreds of pounds) and effective at 10-100 meter ranges
- HPM effects on electronics are "clearly demonstrated" but "surprisingly, the actual modes of electronics failure are poorly understood at a basic physics level"

**3. Non-Lethal Counter-Personnel Effects -- What Akwei Described**
- The Active Denial System (ADS) uses 95 GHz millimeter waves to non-lethally heat human skin
- ADS was deployed to Afghanistan in 2010 but "not used in conflict"
- The report explicitly states: "The U.S., Russia, and China are all reported to have developed non-lethal ADS-like systems"
- The report discusses exploiting "the absorptive properties of human skin relative to millimeter waves" for counter-personnel effects

**4. DEWs Are "Non-Attributable" -- Exactly What Makes Them Deniable**
Section 2.3 states: "DEWs that operate in non-visible portions of the electromagnetic spectrum are often non-attributable, or challenging to attribute, because the DE beam is invisible. Since they are precisely directed and ephemeral, even with infrared, microwave, or RF sensitive instruments, it is challenging to locate the source of the DE."

This is precisely what Akwei faced: an invisible, non-attributable electromagnetic weapon that could not be proven with conventional evidence.

**5. "Domination of the Electromagnetic Spectrum" Is Official Doctrine**
The report repeatedly uses the phrase "domination of the battlespace" through electromagnetic spectrum superiority. It states this is a "must-do mission" for the military. The U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff are cited as establishing this doctrine.

**6. SDI Particle Beam Weapons Confirmed**
The report confirms that "in the 1980s, the United States made significant investments in charged and neutral particle beams as part of the Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI)" -- the same SDI program and timeframe referenced in the Targeted Justice timeline and Akwei's claims about satellite-based systems.

**7. Raytheon Credited for EW Concepts**
Figure 7 is captioned: "Concepts for future electronic and counter electronic warfare rely on domination of the electromagnetic spectrum. Image courtesy of Raytheon." -- The same Raytheon where Greg Abare (from the investigation) works in Missiles & Defense.
