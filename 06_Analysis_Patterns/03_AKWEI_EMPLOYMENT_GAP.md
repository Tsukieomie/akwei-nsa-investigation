## CAPITOL TECHNOLOGY UNIVERSITY -- THE NSA PIPELINE

Capitol College (now Capitol Technology University) is located in **Laurel, Maryland -- 6 miles from NSA headquarters at Fort Meade.**

- Designated an **NSA Center of Academic Excellence** for over 12 years
- Selected by NSA to train NSA security engineers
- Alumni from Akwei's exact cohort (1986-1991 graduates) went directly to:
  - **NSA** (Victor Moxey, 1988)
  - **Booz Allen Hamilton** (Michelle Carrington, 1988)
  - **SAIC** (Jeff Beeson, 1988)
  - **Northrop Grumman** (Regis Brekosky, 1986)
  - **Lockheed Martin**, **Raytheon**, and other defense contractors

An Electronics Technology graduate from Capitol College in 1986 would have been a natural candidate for the NSA contractor ecosystem. His classmates entered it. The question is whether he did too.

## IN FORMA PAUPERIS (1992)

Akwei filed his lawsuit in forma pauperis -- swearing under oath he could not afford the ~$120 filing fee. This means by February 1992:
- He was either impoverished or had very limited income
- This is inconsistent with an active defense contractor position at that time
- It does NOT rule out prior contractor employment that ended before 1992

## THE CAREER DOWNSHIFT

The transition from Electronics Technology degree (1986) to temp agency data management work (1994) represents a significant career downshift. Possible explanations:

1. **Disrupted life:** Consistent with his claims of 10+ years of harassment beginning ~1982
2. **Exited classified work:** Someone leaving a clearance-required position who then becomes adversarial toward their former employer would lose access to the entire defense contractor ecosystem
3. **Mental health:** If his claims reflected a psychotic episode, this could explain career disruption (though his subsequent 30-year career in data science argues against chronic mental illness)
4. **Combination:** Brief contractor exposure, followed by genuine or perceived harassment, leading to financial instability and the IFP lawsuit filing

---

## ASSESSMENT

The hypothesis that Akwei briefly worked in the Fort Meade contractor corridor after his 1986 graduation cannot be confirmed or ruled out. The circumstantial evidence is suggestive:

- His school is 6 miles from NSA and explicitly feeds the NSA workforce
- His classmates entered the NSA/defense contractor pipeline
- His technical education (Electronics Technology) is the exact skill set the NSA contractor ecosystem recruits
- The 8-year gap with zero public trace is unusual for a technical graduate in the nation's most defense-concentrated employment corridor
- His knowledge of NSA organizational terminology goes beyond what public sources alone would provide
- The career downshift to temp work in 1994 is consistent with someone exiting a classified career path

The most parsimonious reading: Akwei likely had some exposure to the NSA ecosystem -- whether as a brief contractor, through social connections with classmates who worked there, or both -- before his life was disrupted by the events he describes in his complaint.
