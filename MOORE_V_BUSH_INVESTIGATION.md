**Date:** March 27, 2026
**Subject:** Independent investigation of Moore v. Bush (1:07-cv-00107-RMC) and the circulation of the Akwei v. NSA dossier as a foundational text within the "targeted individual" community

## Executive Summary

The PDF reviewed — Document 14 from Moore v. Bush et al. — is not just a single pro se filing. It is a window into a documented phenomenon: the Akwei v. NSA Nexus Magazine article functioning as a canonical text that is reproduced, attached as evidence, and cited in federal court filings across the United States by unrelated plaintiffs over a period of at least 20 years. This investigation traces the full lifecycle of the Moore case, identifies at least five additional federal cases where the Akwei material appears, uncovers the 2018 Washington State Fusion Center incident where the same material turned up in government files, and maps the broader pattern of "targeted individual" litigation in federal courts.

## Part 1: The Full Moore v. Bush Case — From Filing to Dismissal

### Case History

John E. Moore filed this case on January 17, 2007 in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia, naming George W. Bush and others as defendants. The case was assigned to Judge Rosemary M. Collyer (Case No. 1:07-CV-00107-RMC).

**Full docket timeline** (reconstructed from [PlainSite](https://www.plainsite.org/courts/district-of-columbia-district-court/moore-v-bush-et-al/1iqen4pe/) and [CaseMine](https://www.casemine.com/judgement/us/5914681fadd7b049342bef97/amp)):

### What Moore Actually Claimed

The [full court opinion](https://www.casemine.com/judgement/us/5914681fadd7b049342bef97/amp) reveals claims far beyond what appeared in Document 14:

- Moore alleged he was "implanted with a micro-chip" for the purpose of controlling his brain
- He claimed to be the victim of a conspiracy to develop and use "brainwave technology" for warrantless surveillance
- He filed FOIA requests with the FBI (Boston, Jacksonville, and Headquarters) and the NSA seeking records about himself
- His NSA requests specifically asked: (1) information about himself in NSA databases, (2) a statement on the existence of "brainwave technology," (3) whether "system instruction was disseminated at Falls Church, VA," and (4) whether a "system detector chip was implanted in the temporal bone area"

### How Each Claim Was Resolved

| Claim | Outcome |
|-------|---------|
| All non-FOIA claims (conspiracy, brainwave technology, Bush as defendant, SPLC as defendant) | Dismissed, Feb 26, 2008 |
| FOIA — FBI Boston | Summary judgment for DOJ. Search adequate. Two pages released with proper redactions |
| FOIA — FBI Jacksonville | Dismissed without prejudice (Moore failed to exhaust administrative remedies; no appeal filed) |
| FOIA — FBI Headquarters | Dismissed without prejudice (Moore failed to exhaust; no appeal filed) |
| FOIA — NSA (records about Moore) | Summary judgment for NSA. Adequate search; Moore never affiliated with NSA; no records found |
| FOIA — NSA (brainwave technology statements) | Denied. FOIA does not require agencies to create documents |
| FOIA — NSA (surveillance/targeting records) | Proper Glomar response under Exemptions 1 and 3 (classified national security; intelligence sources and methods) |

The NSA confirmed that Moore "has never been affiliated with NSA," could not locate any records, and properly declined to confirm or deny any surveillance activity under the Glomar doctrine.

### What We Learn About Moore

- **Full name:** John Edmond Moore (per FBI search variants in the opinion)
- **Address:** 9 Pleasant View Circle, Daytona Beach, FL 32118-5511
- **Property records** ([Homes.com](https://www.homes.com/property/9-pleasant-view-cir-daytona-beach-fl/y31t7slxym8k2/)): John E. Moore and Clara M. Moore owned the property from approximately September 1990 to July 2001. The property was later sold for $310,000 in 2021
- **FBI interview:** Moore referenced a prior FBI interview at the Boston Field Office where he filled out a "personnel information form." The FBI released two pages (with the agent's name redacted). Moore claimed the agent "twisted facts" and described him with "paranoid tendencies"
- **Procedural errors:** Moore failed to appeal FBI denials at two of three offices, failed to properly serve the U.S. Attorney General, and his legal filings contained numerous procedural and formatting errors typical of pro se litigation
- **Pattern:** Moore filed this case against the President, DOJ, NSA, and the SPLC simultaneously — a multi-entity conspiracy claim

## Part 2: The Verified Elements

### The FBI Holocaust Curriculum — Real

Moore's attached news clipping ("FBI uses Holocaust to teach new agents") references a real and well-documented program. FBI Director [Robert Mueller confirmed](https://speakola.com/ideas/robert-mueller-university-of-virginia-jefferson-medal-2013) in a 2013 speech: "In a practice started by my predecessor, Louis Freeh, all new agents visit the Holocaust Museum in Washington to better understand what happens when law enforcement becomes a tool of oppression — or worse."

The [9/11 Memorial Museum](https://www.911memorial.org/connect/blog/911-memorial-museum-visit-added-fbi-agent-and-analyst-training) confirmed that as of Director Wray's tenure, the Holocaust Museum and Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial remain two of only three mandatory training sites for all FBI trainees.

Moore's interpretation of this program — that it proves the FBI recognized the danger of "hate in law enforcement" and thus should have acted on his complaint — is a non sequitur, but the program itself is real.

### Dr. Peter Cariani and Temporal Coding — Real Science, Misapplied

Moore's self-authored research paper (pages 1a-5a) references "Dr. Cariani" and "Temporal Coding of Sensory Information in the Brain." This refers to [Dr. Peter Cariani](http://petercariani.com/Publications_files/CCAI95-EarlyTemporalCodingReview.pdf), a real researcher who was an Assistant Professor in Otology and Laryngology at Harvard Medical School and a Research Associate at the Eaton Peabody Laboratory. His [published work](https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/ast/22/2/22_2_77/_pdf) on temporal coding of sensory information is genuine peer-reviewed neuroscience.

However, Moore's paper takes Cariani's legitimate research on how the brain encodes sensory information and extrapolates it into a framework for externally reproducing brainwave data via ASIC chips — a connection Cariani's research does not support. Moore himself acknowledges: "I have no data on this statement. But a what if?"

### The Falls Church, Virginia Claim — No Independent Evidence

Moore's specific claim that "Brainwave Technology Instruction" was "disseminated at Falls Church, Virginia" to a law enforcement officer could not be independently verified. No evidence exists of any brainwave technology training program for law enforcement at Falls Church. The FBI Academy is located in Quantico, Virginia, not Falls Church. Falls Church is home to various government offices but none linked to brainwave technology instruction.

### The Washington State Fusion Center Incident (2018)

In April 2018, journalist Curtis Waltman of [MuckRock](https://www.muckrock.com/news/archives/2018/apr/18/fusion-center-em/) submitted a FOIA request to the Washington State Fusion Center (WSFC) seeking records on Antifa and white supremacist groups. The WSFC's response inadvertently included a file labeled "EM effects on human body.zip" containing documents on "psycho-electronic weapons," including images and text directly from the Akwei Nexus Magazine article.

As [Yahoo Tech reported](https://tech.yahoo.com/transportation/articles/government-accidentally-sends-docs-mind-135300269.html): "At least some of the images appear to be part of an article in Nexus magazine describing a 1992 lawsuit brought by one John St. Clair Akwei against the NSA."

The WSFC never explained why these documents were in their files. [Analysis](https://mirrorwilderness.com/2018/04/23/fusion-center-accidentally-releases-remote-mind-control-files/) concluded the documents "are obviously not government material" and likely originated from an investigation of someone's beliefs or were submitted by a member of the public. The materials included the same "Psycho-Electronic Weapon Effects" body diagram that appears on page 13c of Moore's filing.

This incident demonstrates that the Akwei material has penetrated not just federal courtrooms but also law enforcement intelligence files — though apparently as collected material rather than as an intelligence product.

## Part 4: The Pattern — "Targeted Individual" Federal Litigation at Scale

### The Scale

Research from the [Forensic Psychiatry Institute](https://www.forensicpsychiatryinstitute.com/neither-gangs-nor-stalking-a-review-of-gang-stalking-allegations-in-the-u-s-federal-court-system/) (2025) reviewed 80 federal cases filed between 2018-2023 containing "gang stalking" allegations. The earlier [NIH study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7178134/) examined 128 cases. These represent only the cases that used specific terminology — the actual number of TI-related federal filings is likely much larger.

| Feature | Moore v. Bush | TI Filing Pattern (N=80) |

### The Caterbone Case — The Most Extreme Example

Stan J. Caterbone of Lancaster, PA represents perhaps the most extensively documented serial TI litigant. Per [court records](https://case-law.vlex.com/vid/stanley-j-caterbone-advanced-892506239), he:
- Filed "numerous civil actions" in the Eastern District of Pennsylvania
- Named the NSA, DARPA, DoD, DIA, CIA, FBI, DOJ, AG, Pennsylvania State Police, local police, and multiple local officials as defendants
- Described himself as a "purported telepath"
- Claimed government handlers "remotely trained" him in mental telepathy
- Alleged connection to "some 20 or so individuals ranging from CIA Operatives to current day national newscasters and celebrities"
- Filed petitions to the [U.S. Supreme Court](https://www.supremecourt.gov/DocketPDF/20/20-5638/162991/20201208111605637_20201208-111432-95752171-00000951.pdf) (cert denied)
- Was placed on notice that "further baseless filings may result in restriction of his filing privileges"
- Receives disability benefits for "symptoms and illnesses related to U.S. Sponsored Mind Control"

The court described Caterbone's filing history as indicating the Eastern District of Pennsylvania was a "hostile environment" — Caterbone's characterization, because courts kept dismissing his claims.

## Part 5: The Akwei Dossier's Role as a "Canonical Text"

### How It Functions

The Akwei Nexus Magazine article serves a specific function within the TI community: it provides a seemingly authoritative, technical-sounding framework that gives names, mechanisms, and institutional attribution to experiences that the individuals interpret as electronic harassment. Its elements include:

1. **Named defendants** (NSA, Kinnecome group) — gives targets for legal action
2. **Specific technology claims** (Remote Neural Monitoring, EMF Brain Stimulation, DOMINT) — provides a vocabulary
3. **A filed court case number** (Civil Action 92-0449) — lends judicial legitimacy
4. **Diagrams and technical charts** — creates an appearance of scientific rigor
5. **A reference list of books** — suggests depth of research

The document circulates through:
- MindControlForums.com (the source URL visible in Moore's printout)
- GangstalkingMindControlCults.com
- American Patriot Friends Network
- Academia.edu
- Reddit communities (r/TargetedSolutions, r/Intelligence)
- Direct attachment to federal court filings

### The Reproduction Pattern

Each plaintiff who attaches the Akwei material to their own filing follows a similar pattern:
1. Experience distressing symptoms (voices, sensations, perceived surveillance)
2. Search for explanations online
3. Discover the Akwei dossier and TI community frameworks
4. Adopt the Akwei vocabulary (Remote Neural Monitoring, V2K, etc.)
5. File a federal lawsuit using the Akwei material as evidence
6. Case is dismissed

This is consistent with the [Psychology Today analysis](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/psych-unseen/202010/gang-stalking-conspiracy-delusion-and-shared-belief) of gang stalking as a phenomenon where "the internet has made it infinitely easier to find people who might share, or claim to share, even the most unconventional beliefs."

## Part 7: The DIA Document — A Legitimate Parallel

One genuinely significant discovery in this investigation is a [Defense Intelligence Agency document](https://www.dia.mil/FOIA/FOIA-Electronic-Reading-Room/FileId/237654/) titled "Anomalous Acute and Subacute Field Effects on Human Biological Tissues" (2009, declassified). This document, produced under the DIA's Advanced Aerospace Weapon System Applications (AAWSA) program, describes:

- Documented injuries to humans from electromagnetic radiation field effects
- Acute effects including erythema, fever, headaches, cardiac palpitations, sleep disturbances, severe anxiety
- The auditory threshold for microwave hearing at 16 mJ/kg
- The "stun effect" threshold at 680 J
- A case where a citizen who "believed he was being targeted" obtained a classified (until 2006) INSCOM document that "described precisely and accurately the nature of the Field Effects" he claimed existed — the government's own psychiatrist diagnosed him with "paranoid schizophrenia," but the document he possessed was real

This last point is striking: a person diagnosed as psychotic possessed a genuine classified document describing the technology he claimed was being used against him. The DIA report notes this as a legitimate intelligence concern, not as validation of the individual's targeting claims.

## Conclusion

The Moore v. Bush filing is a textbook example of the Akwei dossier's role as a foundational text within the targeted individual community. It demonstrates the complete lifecycle: a person experiencing distress discovers the Akwei framework online, adopts its vocabulary and claims, prints the document, attaches it to a federal lawsuit, and has the case dismissed.

The investigation identified at least five other federal cases with the same pattern, spanning 2007-2021 across Florida, Idaho, Pennsylvania, Texas, and the D.C. Circuit. The 2018 Washington State Fusion Center incident shows the material has even entered law enforcement files, though not as an official intelligence product.

The most important finding may be what the Moore case does not contain: any independently verifiable anomaly like the Kinnecome death/employment correlation in the Akwei case. This reinforces the prior investigation's conclusion that while the broader TI phenomenon is best explained by psychiatric research on persecutory beliefs reinforced by online communities, the original Akwei case contains at least one element — the death correlation — that resists simple dismissal.
