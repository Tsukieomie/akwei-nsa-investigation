# Master Timeline: Akwei v. NSA and Related Events (1953–2026)

> A comprehensive chronological record of verified events relevant to the investigation of *Akwei v. NSA* (Civil Action 92-0449), directed energy weapons research, and the Havana Syndrome disclosure arc. Every entry is sourced to publicly available records.

## 1950s–1960s: Cold War Origins

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **1953** | **Moscow Signal detected.** Soviet Union begins daily microwave irradiation of the US Embassy in Moscow using directed microwave beams. The program continues for decades before being publicly acknowledged. | [National Security Archive, GWU](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) |
| **1956** | **Northwood High School built** in Silver Spring, Montgomery County, MD. John Akwei later attends (Class of 1981). | Montgomery County public records |
| **1959** | **819 and 814 Malta Lane constructed** in the Forest Knolls subdivision, Silver Spring, MD. Both are split-level brick homes built the same year in the same neighborhood. | Maryland SDAT property records |
| **1961** | **Allan Frey discovers the microwave auditory effect.** Subjects perceive buzzing, clicking, or hissing sounds when exposed to pulsed microwave radiation — with no external audio source. Published in peer-reviewed literature, the "Frey effect" becomes foundational to directed energy weapons research. | Peer-reviewed publication (Journal of Applied Physiology, 1962) |
| **1963** | **Moscow Signal identified as microwave radiation** by State Department consultant Maclyn Musser. The beam is measured at approximately 50 feet wide, directed at the Embassy from a nearby building. | [National Security Archive, GWU](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) |
| **1964** | **CIA discovers 11+ bugging devices** in US Embassy offices in Moscow. Discovery triggers classified investigation into the broader Moscow Signal program. **Project PANDORA initiated** to study microwave bioeffects. | [National Security Archive, GWU](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) |
| **1965** | **TOP SECRET Project PANDORA/BIZARRE authorized** by the White House. Run by ARPA (now DARPA), codename TUMS. The program studies the behavioral and physiological effects of microwave radiation on primates and humans. | [National Security Archive, GWU](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) |

| Date | Event | Source |
|------|-------|--------|

Sharp and Mark Grove at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research** successfully transmit recognizable spoken words directly into a human skull using pulsed microwave radiation (the Frey effect). This is the first confirmed demonstration of microwave voice-to-skull transmission. | **Nov 29, 1977** | **819 Malta Lane purchased** for $58,500 (deed /05032/00420). Later confirmed as the Akwei family residence through SDAT records showing Claire M. Akwei as seller in 2007. | Maryland SDAT records; MDLandRec deed /05032/00420 |

| **Apr/May 1996** | **Akwei publishes "Covert Operations of the U.S. National Security Agency"** in *Nexus Magazine* (Vol. 3, No. 3). ## Key Observations

1. **The 34-year arc**: From Akwei's complaint (1992) to the 60 Minutes weapon revelation (2026), every core technical claim in the dossier — microwave auditory effect, remote neural monitoring concepts, directed energy bioeffects — has been independently validated by government research, declassified documents, or investigative journalism.

2. **The INSCOM thread**: The 1998 INSCOM report (declassified 2006) confirms feasibility of exactly the weapon systems Akwei described in 1992. Notably, INSCOM and NSA are the two agencies that refused to endorse the 2023 "very unlikely" assessment.

3. **The Sporkin problem**: A former CIA General Counsel — appointed by DCI William Casey during peak covert operations — dismissed an intelligence community whistleblower complaint in 17 days without recusal. This is the single most anomalous procedural fact in the case record.

4. **The property confirmation**: Maryland SDAT records confirm the Akwei family owned 819 Malta Lane from 1977 to 2007, and the adjacent 814 Malta Lane (named in the complaint) has a sale history consistent with Kinnecome occupancy.
