# Technology Claims Framework: Three-Layer Argument Structure

**Compiled: 2026-04-04**
**Purpose: Anchor document for all technology analysis files in this directory**

---

## Why This Document Exists

The technology analysis in this investigation is rigorous and well-sourced. But without a clear framework, two very different types of claims can blur together:

- "This technology existed and was feasible" — arguable from public sources
- "This technology was deployed against Akwei specifically" — not verifiable from public sources

This document defines the three layers of the technology argument and makes explicit which layer each claim belongs to. Every file in `03_Technology_Patents/` should be read through this framework.

---

## The Three Layers

### Layer 1 — Technology Exists
**Claim:** The described capabilities (V2K, remote neural monitoring, through-wall tracking, physiological manipulation) are real phenomena with documented scientific basis and patent records.

**Standard of evidence:** Patent filings, peer-reviewed research, declassified documents, official government reports.

**Status:** ✓ Established. The patent chain (Flanagan → Puharich → Malech → Stocklin → Loos → Norris-Putterman) is real. The Nobel Prize-validated Piezo1 mechanism is real. The Havana Syndrome NAS report is real. The USAF V2K patent (US 6,587,729) is real.

**What this does NOT show:** That the NSA possesses or operates these specific systems. That any government agency has used them against civilians.

---

### Layer 2 — Technology Was Feasible at the Relevant Time and Place
**Claim:** By 1990, a well-resourced intelligence agency with access to NSA computing infrastructure and RF engineering expertise could have operated a system consistent with Akwei's described experience, at the distances involved (15–30 meters, Malta Lane, Wheaton MD).

**Standard of evidence:** Signal budget calculations, historical computing specifications, declassified capability assessments, published RF research from the period.

**Status:** ✓ Argued. The signal budget analysis, NSA Cray Y-MP specifications, and proximity of relevant facilities support the feasibility conclusion. This is an engineering argument, not a factual claim about what happened.

**What this does NOT show:** That the NSA chose to build and operate such a system. That Akwei's home was targeted. That the named defendants participated in any operation.

---

### Layer 3 — Technology Was Deployed Against Akwei Specifically
**Claim:** The NSA actually operated this technology against John S. Akwei at 819 Malta Lane, Wheaton MD, beginning in or around 1990, as described in his 1992 complaint and 1996 Nexus dossier.

**Standard of evidence required:** Classified NSA operational records, FISA court orders, signals intelligence logs, or firsthand testimony from participants.

**Status:** ✗ Not verifiable from public sources. This investigation does not establish Layer 3. It establishes that Layer 3 is not implausible — that the technology and infrastructure existed — but implausibility and proof are different things.

**What would be required to establish this:** Access to NSA records (FOIA, congressional oversight, whistleblower disclosure, or declassification). None of that is available to this investigation.

---

## The Core Argument of This Investigation

This investigation argues:

> "The technology Akwei described was real, was feasible at the relevant time and place, and was consistent with the capabilities of the named institutional actors. The case therefore deserved substantive judicial review rather than a 17-day dismissal as 'frivolous.' Whether the underlying allegations are true requires access to classified records this investigation does not have."

That is a Layer 1 + Layer 2 argument in support of a procedural claim (the dismissal was improper). It is not a Layer 3 argument.

## Language Guide for This Repository

When writing about technology claims, use language that reflects which layer you are working in:

### Layer 1 language (appropriate)
- "The patent demonstrates..."
- "Published research confirms..."
- "The Flanagan Neurophone proves that coupling of this kind exists..."
- "The USAF patented V2K capability in 2003..."

### Layer 2 language (appropriate)
- "If such a system were operated at 15–30 meters, the signal budget would support..."
- "A hypothetical operation consistent with Akwei's account would require..."
- "The feasibility analysis shows this would have been within reach of NSA infrastructure in 1990..."
- "The technology existed and would have been capable of producing the described effects..."

### Language that conflates Layer 2 and Layer 3 (avoid)
- "The operation was conducted from 814 Malta Lane..." → *asserts Layer 3 as fact*
- "NSA deployed this against Akwei..." → *asserts Layer 3 as fact*
- "This is a blueprint for Akwei's RNM system..." → *implies Layer 3 without evidence*
- "Akwei experienced two-way RNM contact..." → *takes Akwei's account as established fact*
- "The equipment next door..." → *asserts Layer 3 as fact*

### Preferred framing for borderline cases
- "If Akwei's account is accurate, the equipment would have been..."
- "Consistent with Akwei's description of two-way contact..."
- "Akwei alleged / Akwei claimed / Akwei described..."
- "The hypothetical operation Akwei describes would be consistent with..."
